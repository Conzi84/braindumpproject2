9:T57c,<h1>Risk Management Frameworks</h1>
<h2>What is Risk Management?</h2>
<p>At its core: identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to capital and earnings.</p>
<p>In energy: equipment failure, regulatory changes, commodity price volatility.</p>
<p>In trading: position sizing, drawdown limits, correlation exposure.</p>
<h2>The Four Pillars</h2>
<h3>1. Identification</h3>
<ul>
<li>What can go wrong?</li>
<li>Run scenario analysis</li>
<li>Map dependencies</li>
</ul>
<h3>2. Assessment</h3>
<ul>
<li>Likelihood √ó Impact matrix</li>
<li>Quantify where possible</li>
<li>Acknowledge uncertainty</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Mitigation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hedge exposures</li>
<li>Diversify sources</li>
<li>Build redundancy</li>
</ul>
<h3>4. Monitoring</h3>
<ul>
<li>Real-time dashboards</li>
<li>Alert thresholds</li>
<li>Regular reviews</li>
</ul>
<h2>Application to AI Projects</h2>
<p>Same framework applies:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Identify</strong>: What if the model hallucinates critical data?</li>
<li><strong>Assess</strong>: How often? What's the business impact?</li>
<li><strong>Mitigate</strong>: Human review layers, confidence thresholds</li>
<li><strong>Monitor</strong>: Track accuracy over time</li>
</ul>
<h2>Why This Matters</h2>
<p>Risk management isn't about avoiding risk‚Äîit's about taking calculated risks with eyes open.</p>
<p>Energy, trading, AI... same principles, different variables.</p>
